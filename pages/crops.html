Peanuts

The company cultivates peanuts in East Oweinat and Nubaria and works in partnerships with farmers and major agricultural companies in Egypt in order to obtain the highest quality and so that the final product conforms to the international specifications familiar to our clients abroad.

The crop is then transferred to our factories, where the cycle of preparing the crop and preparing it for export abroad begins through the following stages:

First - the first cleaning stage:- At this stage, the crop is cleaned of impurities and dust

Secondly - the stage of sizing scales:- At this stage, the sizes are determined according to the specifications of each customer

The third stage of screening:- The crop is then sorted through the manual and automatic sorting stages using trained technical workers and through the latest advanced sorting machines (Sortex).

Fourth - the stage of packaging: - In this final stage, the peanuts are packed in sacks and jumbo bags prepared for this purpose and in conformity with European specifications and loaded into containers after issuing certificates and papers for the shipment and monitoring the concerned authorities from the Egyptian state and following up the matter until the port of our customers abroad

okra

Okra is low in calories but packed full of nutrients. The vitamin C in okra helps support healthy immune function. Okra is also rich in vitamin K, which helps your body clot blood.

green peas

Peas are in the group of foods known as legumes. Legumes are plants that produce pods with seeds, or beans, inside. Other foods in the legume family include lentils, soybeans, chickpeas, and all types of beans.

A ½ cup serving of green peas (about a handful) contains:

pomegranate

A pomegranate is a sweet, tart fruit with thick, red skin. While the skin is not edible, it holds hundreds of juicy seeds that you can eat plain or sprinkle on salads, oatmeal, hummus, and other dishes. Bottled pomegranate juice is also an easy way to enjoy some of the health benefits of this delicious fruit.

Half of a pomegranate is one serving and contains:

  • Carbohydrates: 14 grams
  • Fiber: 3 grams
  • Sugar: 11 grams
